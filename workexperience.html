Tufts HRI Lab

I worked for the Tufts Human Robot Interactions Lab, HRI for short,as an undergraduate research assistant over the summer of 2016. I focused on research and assisted graduate student Bradley Oosterveld in developing speech recognition software and intergrating it with Nao robots. I worked on a speech segmentation module that analyzes audio and breaks it into individual segments trimmed of non-speech noise. We also worked on integrating Bradley's audio recognition functions into an external module for Nao robots. I primarily coded using C++ with various SDK's and worked within the larger Tufts HRI enviroment code base.
